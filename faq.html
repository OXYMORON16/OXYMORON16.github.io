FAQ

1What is waste management?

Waste management refers to the process of collecting, treating, and disposing of waste in a safe and efficient manner, with the goal of reducing waste, promoting recycling, and protecting the environment.

2 What are the different types of waste?

There are several types of waste, including household, hazardous, e-waste, construction & demolition, and agricultural waste.

3What is the role of waste management in protecting the environment?

Waste management plays an important role in protecting the environment by reducing the amount of waste that is sent to landfills, which can produce greenhouse gases and contaminate soil and groundwater.

4 What are the benefits of recycling?

Recycling conserves natural resources, reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills, and helps to mitigate the environmental impacts of waste disposal.

5 What is composting?

Composting is the process of breaking down organic waste materials, such as food scraps and yard waste, into a nutrient-rich soil amendment.

6 What is the difference between single-stream and dual-stream recycling?

Single-stream recycling refers to a system in which all recyclable materials are collected in one container, while dual-stream recycling involves separating recyclables into two separate containers, such as paper and containers.

7 How can I recycle properly at home?

Proper recycling at home involves researching your local recycling program, sorting recyclables into the correct categories, and ensuring that items are clean and free of contaminants before recycling.
